We want our clients to have all the information necessary to make an informed and intelligent decision, so we put together a short list of points to take into consideration when choosing a remodeling contractor.

1. Check His References
Inquire about the contractor's previous projects so you know what their quality of work is like.  Be sure to ask for references that will be able to tell you things like how quickly the job was completed and if the contractor was clean and courteous.

2. Do Not Make it A Do-It-Yourself Job
Smaller jobs, like painting, are ok for the homeowner to tackle, but anything larger than that will most likely require a qualified professional.  Less than 20% of do-it-yourself jobs turn out well and when you do it yourself, you most likely will have to call a professional anyway to fix the problem.

3. Put it in Writing
Write up a contract that goes over everything about the project: proof of insurance, schedule of payments, estimated start and end date of the project, products and materials that are going to be used, etc.  Insisting that there is a clear contract is not a sign of mistrust, its a sing that you want your renovation to be successful.

4. Don't Automatically Go With the Low-Ball Offer
Just because a contractor has the lowest price doesn't mean he is the best bet.  Other things to take into consideration are quality of work, how well he communicates and how highly his past clients speak of him.
