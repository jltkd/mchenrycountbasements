We want all of our clients to have relevant information so they can make an intelligent and informed decision about who they choose for their remodel, so we put together a couple points to keep in mind.

4. Don't Just Go With the Low-Ball Offer
You usually just want to not consider the low-ball offer.  It usually translates to the contractor not being very good or is desperate for work, both of these can translate into a less-than-favorable contractor experience.  Use other factors like quality of work and experience to make your decision.

3. Investigate
Do proper research.  Make calls to previous clients to check how their projects went.  Pay a visit to one of their current sites, if at all possible.  If you see the job site is clean and workers are courteous, the contractor is probably a safe bet.

3. Get Everything in Writing
Get a contract written up that details each and every aspect of the project.  Getting a clear contract in writing isn't an indicator you don't trust the contractor, it simply means you want a successful remodeling project.

2. Several Years of Experience Doesn't Equal Quality
There are a lot of people that get poor work from contractors who say they have been in bbusiness for 20+ years. This doesn't mean a lot because they could have just done bad jobs for all those twenty years. Make sure to take into consideration other factors to decide if the contractor is a qualified professional.
