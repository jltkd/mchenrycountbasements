We want all of our clients to have the information necessary to make an informed and intelligent purchase decision, so we have made a list of points to take into consideration when deciding on a remodeling contractor.

2. Do Not Make it A Do-It-Yourself Job
Smaller jobs, like painting, are ok for the homeowner to tackle, but anything larger than that will most likely require a qualified professional.  Less than 20% of do-it-yourself jobs go well and when you do it yourself, you most likely will have to call a professional to fix the problem anyway.

1. Check His References
Ask about projects the contractor has had in the past so you know what their quality of work is like.  Make sure to get references that will be able to tell you things like how clean the contractor was and if they completed it within the given timeframe.

4. Don't Automatically Go With the Low-Ball Offer
Just because a contractor has the lowest price doesn't mean he is the best one to go with.  Other things to take into consideration are quality of work, how well he communicates and what his references say about him.

3. Put it in Writing
Write up a contract that goes over everything about the project: schedule of payments, products and materials that are going to be used, estimated start and end date of the project, proof of insurance, etc.  Making sure there is a clear contract doesn't mean you don't trust the contractor, it only means you want a successful renovation.
