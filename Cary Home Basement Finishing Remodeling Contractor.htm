We want all of our clients to have relevant information so they can make an intelligent and informed decision about who they choose for their remodel, so we put together a couple points to keep in mind.

2. Many Years of Experience Doesn't Equal Quality
There are so many people that get poor work from contractors who claim to have been in the business or trade for 20+ years. This doesn't mean much because they could have just done bad work for all those twenty years. Make sure to look at other factors to determine if the contractor is a qualified professional.

1. Check References
Take a look at the contractor's past projects so you have a good idea of their work quality.  Get references from them and be sure to ask those references pertinent questions like if the project was completed under budget and how clean and tidy they were.

4. Don't Immediately Take the Low-Ball Offer
This doesn't necessarily mean that the one with the low-ball offer is not experienced, but that should only be one factor you take into consideration when choosing.  If the contractor with the lowest offer also happens to be the most qualified, then go with him.

3. Get Everything in Writing
Get a contract written up that goes over each and every detail of the project.  Getting a clear contract in writing doesn't mean you don't trust the contractor, it just means you want a successful remodel.
