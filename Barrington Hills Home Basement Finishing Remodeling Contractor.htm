We are always looking to provide our clients with an excellent experience and enough relevant information to make an informed and intelligent decision, so we have put together a short list of things to take into consideration when looking to hire a remodeling contractor.

2. Don't Do It Yourself
There are times when a homeowner can do small jobs themselves like small repairs, painting etc, but do not try to take on larger, more complicated projects by yourself.  Usually the job gets botched and you will have to pay more to have the job fixed anyway.  Less than 20% of these jobs turn out as expected. Call a qualified professional if you want to ensure that the job is done correctly.

4. Don't let price be your guide
After you determine your contractor has technical competence, you must make sure you are comfortable and can communicate effectively with him.  Being able to communicate effectively with your contractor is the single most important factor in choosing who you go with.

3. Investigate the Facts
Make sure to research.  Call previous clients and ask how the project went.  If you can, visit a current job site and see how the contractor conducts business.  Look to see if the job site is clean and safe and the workers take care with the homeowners property.

1. Check References
Ask to see the contractor's previous projects so you can get an idea of their quality of work.  If you approve, ask for references and call former customers to check.  Ask things like how the contractor executed the projects and if they were on time and budget.
