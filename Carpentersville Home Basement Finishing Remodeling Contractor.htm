We want all our clients to have every piece of relevant information necessary to make an intelligent and informed decision, so we have put together a list of points to take into consideration when deciding on a remodeling contractor.

1. Check References
Look at the contractor's past projects so you have an idea of their quality of work.  Ask for references and be sure to ask those references pertinent questions like if the project was completed under budget.

2. Many Years of Experience Doesn't Equal Quality
I can't tell you how many people get poor work from contractors who claim to have been in the business or trade for five, ten or twenty or more years. Don't pay too much attention to years of experience, even if they've been in business for twenty years, they could have done a bad job all those twenty years.  Investigate to ensure you are doing business with a qualified professional.

3. Get Everything in Writing
Get a contract written up that goes over every detail of the project: schedule of payments, proof of insurance, products and materials that are going to be used, estimated start and end date of the project, etc.  Having a clear contract doesn't mean you don't trust the contractor, it just means you want your remodeling project to be a success.

4. Don't Automatically Go With the Low-Ball Offer
You usually just want to throw out the low-ball offer.  It usually means that the contractor is not very good or desperate for work, both of these can translate to a not-so-good remodeling experience.  Use other factors like experience and quality of work to make your decision.
